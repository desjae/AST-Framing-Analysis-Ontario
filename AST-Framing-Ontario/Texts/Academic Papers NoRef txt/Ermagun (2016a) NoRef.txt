                                                                                                                                                                            Alireza Ermagun, S.M.ASCE 1; and Amir Samimi 2
                                                                                                                                  Abstract: A growing number of parents accompany their children to and from school, to make sure they travel with the best care and
                                                                                                                                  minimum stress. Policies that aim at promoting nonmotorized modes of transportation or at least discouraging auto driving solely for
                                                                                                                                  the purpose of picking up or dropping off the kids may not be successful in practice unless primary concerns of parents are treated ap-
                                                                                                                                  propriately. School escorting decisions need to be investigated, as a decreasing trend in students’ travel freedom also decreases the tendency
